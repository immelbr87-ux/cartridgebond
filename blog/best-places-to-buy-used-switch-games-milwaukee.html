Best Places to Buy Used Switch Games in Milwaukee

Local game shops, online options, and meetup hotspots. The Milwaukee Switch buyer's guide.

March 28, 2026 5 min read

You want a used Nintendo Switch game in Milwaukee. You want to pay a fair price, not get burned by a bad copy, and not spend an hour coordinating a meetup with someone who ghosts you. Here's where to shop, ranked honestly.

Option 1: CartridgeBond (Best Value + Lowest Risk)

CartridgeBond is built specifically for this use case in Milwaukee. You submit which game you want and what you're willing to pay. We find a local seller whose price and condition align with your request. You meet locally, inspect the game before paying, and complete the exchange.

The price you see when you submit is the price you pay. No bidding, no negotiation at the meetup, no surprise. A1 condition is required for all listings - original case, plays perfectly.

Current free beta pricing: $36-50 depending on title.

Option 2: Facebook Marketplace (Most Inventory, Most Hassle)

Facebook Marketplace has the largest local inventory in Milwaukee of any option. The downside is everything else: you'll receive lowball offers on your own listings, you'll need to filter through "is this still available?" messages, and there's no condition standard - "good condition" means whatever the seller decides it means.

For buyers, the strategy is: search specifically (include "Nintendo Switch" in your search), filter by distance, sort by date listed, and only message sellers who include clear photos of the actual cartridge and case. Expect to negotiate down from their asking price.

Option 3: GameStop (Highest Price, Most Reliable)

GameStop's used games are refurbished, come with a 30-day return policy, and are available immediately in-store. You'll pay a premium for that convenience - typically 20-30% more than a private sale. For common titles like Mario Kart or Animal Crossing, paying GameStop's used price when a private sale exists nearby leaves real money on the table.

Worth considering if: you want the return policy protection, you need it today and can't wait for a match, or you're buying a title not available in the local private market.

Option 4: eBay (Widest Selection, National Reach)

eBay is the right call when you need a specific game that's not widely available in Milwaukee - older titles, limited editions, import games. For common Switch titles, you'll pay more than a local private sale once you factor in shipping and the seller's built-in fee markup.

Buyer tip: always filter by "Sold listings" first to understand real transaction prices, not just wishful asking prices. Filter by "US only" unless you're comfortable with longer shipping times and occasional import issues.

Option 5: r/gameswap and Gaming Facebook Groups

Reddit's r/gameswap and local Milwaukee gaming Facebook groups have active communities of buyers and sellers. Prices tend to be fair (the communities police obvious gouging), and sellers often have documented transaction histories. The process is more manual than any platform - you're DMing individuals, arranging your own meetups, and there's no formal condition standard. But for motivated buyers who want specific titles, it's worth checking.

Buyer's rule of thumb: For common Milwaukee-area titles, CartridgeBond or Facebook Marketplace will find you something faster. For rare or specific titles, eBay or r/gameswap is where to look.
