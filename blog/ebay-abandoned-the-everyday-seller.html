eBay Abandoned the Everyday Seller. Here's What We Built.

May 18, 20264 min read

A piece circulating this week surfaced a number worth sitting with: eBay collected $2 billion last year from sellers paying to promote their own listings on a platform those sellers already pay fees to use.

The piece traces how it happened. A new CEO arrived in 2020 with a strategy called focused categories. Sneakers. Watches. Trading cards. Auto parts. The everyday seller was quietly moved aside. The customer became whoever would pay to be seen.

For Nintendo Switch game sellers, the math is especially rough. You list a game. You pay selling fees. If you want buyers to actually find it among millions of listings, you pay again. By the time a $50 game sells, you are looking at $27 to $34 on a good day.
