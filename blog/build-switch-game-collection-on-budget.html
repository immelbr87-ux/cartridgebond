Build a Switch Game Collection on a Budget

March 28, 2026  ·  6 min read

The Nintendo Switch library is enormous and getting every game you want at full retail prices adds up fast. A strategic used game buying approach in Milwaukee can cut your total spend by 30-50% without compromising on the titles you actually want.

The Used Game Value Proposition

Used Switch games in the local Milwaukee market trade at roughly 60-75% of new retail price for popular titles - and significantly lower for older releases. On five games that retail for $60 each, buying used locally saves you $75-120 compared to buying new from Nintendo or Best Buy. That's a meaningful amount of money for the same gaming experience.

Unlike buying used cars or electronics, used Switch cartridges have essentially no quality degradation - they either work perfectly or they don't, and you can verify this in 60 seconds at the meetup.

The Smart Buying Order

Build your collection strategically - not based on what you want most, but on what represents the best value at the current moment:

  1. Buy titles that just had a price drop - watch for when a series sequel is announced; previous entries often drop 20-30% immediately
  2. Buy older titles first at their floor price - Animal Crossing at $36 is near its floor. It's unlikely to drop much further, so buying now vs later doesn't matter much. Spend more time evaluating titles that are still near their peak.
  3. Buy multiplayer titles early - Mario Kart and Smash are worth buying early in your Switch ownership because you'll get more out of them with more years of play

The Rotation Strategy

The most cost-efficient approach to Switch game ownership is rotation: buy used, play, sell at a similar price, buy something else. If you buy Animal Crossing for $36 locally, play it for three months, and sell it for $34, your effective cost was $2 for three months of entertainment. That's not realistic every time, but the directional math is real.
