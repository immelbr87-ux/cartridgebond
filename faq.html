Frequently Asked Questions

How CartridgeBond works, what to expect, and what we can't do for you.
How It Works
CartridgeBond matches local Nintendo Switch game buyers and sellers. You submit what you have (or want), lock in your price and condition terms, and we find someone compatible in your area. Once we have a match, we connect both parties by email or DM and you coordinate a local meetup. We don't hold money, we don't ship anything, and we're not at the meetup — we just make the introduction.
We review every submission within 24–48 hours. If we have a match available, we'll reach out right away. If not, your submission stays active for the duration of your stated window (0–90 days for Fast Flip, 90–180 days for Flexible). We'll check back in if something comes up — you don't need to resubmit.

We'll send you a note letting you know, and you're welcome to resubmit any time. We're beta, which means our pool of buyers and sellers is still growing. The best thing you can do to help is share the link with friends who have Switch games.

You can submit other Switch titles using the "type a title" option — we'll do our best to match them, though our pool is smaller for unlisted games. More titles and platforms are coming soon.
Pricing & Payment
For our listed titles, we set reference prices based on current secondary market rates — what games actually sell for between private parties, not GameStop trade-in values or eBay auction highs. Sellers lock in a guaranteed price at submission. Buyers know exactly what they'll pay before they ever leave the house. There's no bidding, no counter-offers, no haggling at the meetup.
Payment is handled directly between you and the buyer at the meetup. CartridgeBond doesn't collect or transfer money. Common methods are cash, Venmo, or PayPal. Agree on the payment method with your buyer before you meet. We recommend confirming this when we introduce you — before you leave the house.

GameStop trade-in prices are set to allow them to resell at a significant markup. Our prices reflect what real buyers are willing to pay in a private sale — typically 30–60% more than a GameStop trade-in offer for the same game. We're not a middleman buying to resell, so the spread goes to the seller, not a retailer.
Condition & Disputes
A1 is our current (and only) condition tier. It means: the game plays perfectly, original case is included, the cartridge has no scratches affecting gameplay, and all original components are present. Minor cosmetic surface scuffs are acceptable. If you're not sure whether your game qualifies, describe its condition in the notes field — we'll let you know before we match you.
Inspect the game before paying. This is the most important rule of every CartridgeBond meetup. If the condition doesn't match what was agreed, you're not obligated to complete the exchange. Walk away. CartridgeBond is not a party to the transaction and cannot mediate or resolve condition disputes after payment has been made. See our Safe Meetup Guide for the full inspection checklist.
Let us know by replying to your match email. No-shows affect a user's standing in the system — we track this and factor it into future matches. You're welcome to resubmit and we'll prioritize your match. We're building a rating system to make reliability visible before you ever meet.
Safety & Privacy
When we introduce a match, we share first name and general zip code area only. Full email addresses are shared only in the introduction email so you can coordinate the meetup. We never share phone numbers without consent. See our Privacy Policy for the full picture.
Local exchanges are generally safe when done correctly. Our Safe Meetup Guide covers everything — meet in public, inspect before paying, tell someone where you're going. Many police departments also offer their lobbies as exchange zones. The risk is real but manageable with the right habits.
