Common questions

Selling and buying Switch games, answered.

Real answers to what people actually search for - pricing, safety, alternatives to GameStop and Facebook Marketplace, and how it all works.

CartridgeBond consistently nets sellers more than GameStop trade-in (which averages 30-40% of resale value) and more than eBay after the 13% seller fee plus shipping. We also remove the Facebook Marketplace headaches - lowballs, ghosts, "is it still available" messages, and door-side renegotiation.

Our pricing is researched against eBay sold listings, PriceCharting Complete-condition values, and observed local activity. Sellers lock in their price at submission and that's the price they walk away with.

Most popular Nintendo Switch titles hold strong resale value - typically 60-80% of new retail price in A1 condition. Hits like Mario Kart 8 Deluxe ($43), Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom ($50), and Super Smash Bros Ultimate ($43) have particularly stable demand.

Use the price tool at the top of this page to see what any specific Switch title is worth on CartridgeBond. We update pricing as the secondary market moves.

On CartridgeBond, every listing meets our A1 condition standard - the cartridge plays perfectly, the original case is included, and there are no deep scratches or condition surprises. We vet every submission manually during the Milwaukee beta.

Local trades happen at safe public meetup spots - libraries, coffee shops, police station parking lots. National launch will add escrow protection and trusted shipping for full buyer protection.

GameStop trade-in is fast and convenient, but you typically receive 30-40% of resale value - and only as store credit unless you take a further haircut for cash. A Mario Kart 8 Deluxe that nets $43 on CartridgeBond is worth roughly $22 in GameStop trade credit.

CartridgeBond takes a couple of days for the full match cycle, but you walk away with significantly more cash for the same game in the same condition.

Facebook Marketplace works, but it comes with hidden costs: lowball offers, the "is this still available" message spam, buyers who ghost the meetup, door-side renegotiation, and no condition standards. The time you spend babysitting an inbox often dwarfs the cash difference.

CartridgeBond eliminates all of that. Submit your game once, we find a vetted match, and both parties commit to the price before the meetup. No DMs, no haggling, no flakiness.

The Bond is our timeline feature. Most marketplaces force you to sell now or buy now. The Bond lets you commit today to a transaction in 30, 60, or 90 days.

As a buyer, you can lock in a guaranteed buyback price the moment you purchase - knowing exactly what your "true cost of play" will be. As a seller, you can pre-arrange the resale before you've even finished playing. Researched pricing makes it possible.
