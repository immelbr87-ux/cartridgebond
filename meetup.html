Safe Meetup Guide

Read this before your first exchange. Your safety is your responsibility — we're here to help.

Important: CartridgeBond connects buyers and sellers but is not present at meetups, does not supervise exchanges, and is not responsible for your personal safety or the outcome of any transaction. Meeting a stranger always carries risk. These guidelines are designed to minimize that risk — but following them does not guarantee safety. Use your judgment.

The Rules
📍
Meet in a public place
Coffee shops, libraries, grocery store parking lots, and police station lobbies are ideal. Never meet at someone's home for a first exchange — public visibility is your best protection.
👁️
Inspect before you pay
Never hand over money before you've tested the cartridge. Power on a Switch and verify the game launches. A1 condition means no scratches affecting gameplay and original case included — verify this on the spot.
👥
Bring a friend if you can
Two people at a meetup is always better than one. If you're going alone, tell someone where you're going and who you're meeting. Share your location on your phone.
💵
Agree on payment method in advance
Cash, Venmo, and PayPal are common. Avoid any payment method you can't reverse for a buyer (like Zelle to a stranger). Agree on what you're using before you leave the house.
🚫
Trust your gut
If something feels wrong when you arrive — the person, the location, the item — it's completely okay to walk away. No game is worth your safety. You owe no one an exchange.
📱
Keep your phone charged
Have your phone at full charge before you go. Keep CartridgeBond's contact info saved so you can reach us if something goes wrong with the match details.
Good Meetup Spots in Milwaukee & North Shore
📚 Public Library
Mequon, Glendale, Shorewood, Whitefish Bay branches — well-lit, staffed, public
☕ Starbucks / Panera
Any busy location — cameras, staff, and witnesses nearby
🏪 Grocery Parking Lot
Pick Piggly Wiggly, Sendik's, or any large lot — daytime preferred
🚔 Police Station Lobby
Many WI police stations offer their lobbies as exchange zones — safest option
🎮 GameStop
Ironic but effective — staffed retail location, cameras, familiar environment
🛒 Target / Best Buy
Customer service desks near the entrance are a natural, safe handoff point
What to Check at the Meetup
1
Check the cartridge physically
No cracks, chips, or significant scratches. Label should be intact and legible. Pins should be clean — no corrosion.
2
Test it in a Switch
Insert the cartridge and confirm the game launches. Don't skip this step — "it works great" isn't enough. See it boot.
3
Check the case
A1 condition requires the original case. Verify it's present, undamaged, and contains the correct game.
4
Confirm the agreed price
Both parties should verbally confirm the price before payment. If the seller tries to change the price at the meetup, you are not obligated to proceed.
5
Complete payment
Only exchange payment once you're satisfied with the item. Once payment is made, CartridgeBond has no role in any dispute.
